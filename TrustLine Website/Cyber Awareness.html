Cyber Awareness

Security habits that actually stick

Security does not have to be complicated. Small habits used every day at work and in daily life can make a big difference in keeping you and your team safe online. Start simple, stay consistent and you will build stronger protection over time.

View Tips

Think Before You Click

  • Got an email that feels a bit off? Trust that instinct and take a second look before you click anything
  • Check the full sender address, not just the display name, since it's easy to spoof what shows up there
  • Watch out for urgency. Phrases like "act now" or "your account will be closed" are classic pressure tactics
  • Not sure if it's legit? Ask a colleague or flag it to IT rather than guessing. Better safe than sorry

Better Password Habits

  • Three random words strung together, like TigerGlassMountain makes a password that's both strong and actually memorable
  • Reusing passwords is one of the riskiest habits around. One breach can quickly cascade into several
  • Steer clear of names, birthdays or anything someone could find on your social profiles
  • A password manager handles the hard part for you. Worth setting up if you haven't already

Strengthening Account Security with MFA

  • Turn on MFA for every account that supports it, especially email and anything work-related
  • Even if someone gets hold of your password, MFA usually stops them cold
  • Getting a login code you didn't ask for? That's a sign someone's trying to get in, so don't approve it

Keeping Systems Up to Date

  • Yes, updates are disruptive, but they're often patching real security holes that attackers already know about
  • Don't keep snoozing them, especially on work devices
  • Keeping your browser and apps current is one of the simplest wins available to you, and it works

Using Public Wi-Fi Safely

  • Public Wi-Fi is convenient but that coffee shop network isn't something you should fully trust
  • Avoid logging into work systems or anything sensitive while connected to it
  • If you work remotely often, your company should have a VPN so use it

Lock Your Screen

  • Stepping away from your desk? Lock your screen first. Windows key + L on Windows, or Command + Control + Q on Mac
  • It takes one second and stops anyone walking past from seeing or touching what's open
  • Matters even more in open offices or shared spaces where you don't know everyone around you

Handle Data with Care

  • Share files with the people who actually need them, not a wider group just to cover all bases
  • Before hitting send, double-check the recipient. Autocomplete gets it wrong more often than you'd think
  • Personal devices aren't held to the same security standard as work ones, so be careful what you store on them

Speak Up Early

  • Noticed something strange? Report it early, even if you're not 100% sure it's actually a problem
  • That could be a suspicious email, an unexpected pop-up, or a device you can't locate
  • The sooner something gets flagged, the easier it is to contain, so don't wait until you're certain
